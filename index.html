Lionel Messi

The man who redefined the beautiful game

Lionel Messi seen lifting the FIFA World Cup trophy, surrounded by teammates and fans in celebration. Lionel Messi, front and center, inspires generations with his unmatched skill, humility, and relentless pursuit of excellence – a journey from Rosario’s dusty streets to football’s highest peak.

Here's a timeline of Messi's life:

  • 1987 – Born in Rosario, Argentina
  • 1995 – Joins Newell’s Old Boys, a local youth club, showing early signs of genius
  • 2000 – Diagnosed with a growth hormone deficiency; family moves to Spain so he can join FC Barcelona, who offer to pay for his treatment
  • 2004 – Makes his official debut for FC Barcelona’s senior team at age 17
  • 2005 – Scores his first senior goal for Barcelona and wins the FIFA World Youth Championship with Argentina, finishing as top scorer and best player
  • 2009 – Wins his first Ballon d'Or as the best player in the world, beginning a new era in football
  • 2011 – Plays a key role in Barcelona’s legendary team under Pep Guardiola, considered one of the greatest club teams in history
  • 2012 – Breaks Gerd Müller’s 40-year-old record by scoring 91 goals in a single calendar year, a feat unmatched in modern football
  • 2014 – Leads Argentina to the FIFA World Cup Final, wins the Golden Ball as the tournament's best player
  • 2021 – Leaves Barcelona due to financial constraints, joins Paris Saint-Germain (PSG); wins his 7th Ballon d'Or, the most by any player in history
  • 2022 – Captains Argentina to World Cup glory in Qatar, achieving the final jewel in his crown. Wins the Golden Ball again, the only player to win it twice.
  • 2023 – Joins Inter Miami in Major League Soccer (MLS), boosting football’s popularity in the U.S.
  • 2024 – Continues to inspire young players around the world, both on and off the pitch

    • "Messi is not just a player. He’s a once-in-a-century genius who brought joy, magic, and poetry to football." — Football pundit Gary Lineker
